10.2 ASA and AAS Triangle Congruence

Big Picture

The Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) and Angle-Angle-Side (AAS) criteria are used to prove the congruence of two triangles. The ASA criterion states that if two triangles have two pairs of congruent angles and the common side of those angles is congruent in both triangles, the triangles are congruent. Similarly, the AAS criterion asserts that if two triangles have two pairs of congruent angles and the opposite side of the angles is congruent, the triangles are congruent as well. These criteria help us establish when triangles are identical in size and shape.

Key Terms

  • ASA: Angle-Side-Angle, a criterion for triangle congruence where two angles and the included side are congruent in both triangles.
  • Triangle Congruence: The condition where two triangles have identical size and shape, meaning their corresponding sides and angles are congruent.
  • Congruent: Figures that are identical in size, shape, and measure, even if one is rotated or reflected.
  • Corresponding Angle Pairs: Pairs of angles that are in the same relative position in two different triangles.
  • Angles: A geometric figure formed by two rays (sides) meeting at a single point (vertex).
  • Triangle: A polygon with three sides and three angles.
  • Vertices: The points where two or more sides of a polygon meet.
  • Rigid Transformation: A transformation that preserves the size and shape of a figure, including translation, rotation, and reflection.
  • Distance: The length between two points in space.
  • SAS: Side-Angle-Side, another triangle congruence criterion where two sides and the included angle are congruent in both triangles.

Example of ASA and AAS Congruence

Consider two triangles with the following properties:

  • Triangle 1: Angle A = Angle B = 40°, Side AB = Side BC = 5 units
  • Triangle 2: Angle A' = Angle B' = 40°, Side A'B' = Side B'C' = 5 units

In this case, both triangles have two pairs of congruent angles (Angle A = Angle A' and Angle B = Angle B') and the included sides AB and A'B' are congruent. Therefore, by the ASA criterion, the two triangles are congruent.

Key Takeaways

Understanding the ASA and AAS criteria for triangle congruence is essential for proving that two triangles are identical in size and shape. These criteria rely on the relationships between angles and sides within the triangles. The ASA criterion uses two congruent angles and the included side, while the AAS criterion uses two congruent angles and the opposite side. Mastery of these concepts allows for precise geometric proofs and applications.
