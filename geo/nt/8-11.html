8.11 Surface Area - Nets

Summary Statement of Topic

Surface area is the total area that covers the outside of a three-dimensional object. It can be calculated by using specific formulas based on the shape of the object. Nets provide a way to visualize the surface area by "unfolding" the solid into a 2D shape.

Key Terms

  • Surface Area: The combined total area of all faces of a 3D object.
  • Face: A flat surface on a solid figure.
  • Prism: A 3D figure with 2 congruent bases in parallel planes and rectangular faces.
  • Pyramid: A 3D object with a polygonal base and triangular faces meeting at a single vertex.
  • Net: A diagram showing a “flattened” representation of a solid, including all its faces and dimensions.
  • Edge: A line segment where two faces of a solid intersect.
  • Corresponding: Sides or angles that are in the same relative position in similar geometric figures.
  • Triangular Prism: A prism with two triangular bases and three rectangular faces.
  • Lateral Faces: Non-base faces of a prism or pyramid.
  • Rectangular Prism: A prism with rectangular bases and rectangular faces.
  • Cone: A 3D figure with a circular base and one vertex.
  • Proportion: A statement that two fractions are equal.
  • Circle: A set of points equidistant from a center point.
  • Radius: The distance from the center to the outer rim of a circle.
  • Equilateral Triangle: A triangle with all sides equal in length.
  • Triangle: A polygon with three sides and three angles.
  • Height: The perpendicular distance from the base to the opposite vertex of a triangle.
  • Right Triangle: A triangle with a 90-degree angle.
  • Hypotenuse: The longest side of a right triangle, opposite the right angle.

Example of Surface Area Calculation

Let's calculate the surface area of a rectangular prism. The surface area of a rectangular prism is calculated using the formula:

Surface Area of a Rectangular Prism: A = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

Suppose the length (l) is 4 units, the width (w) is 3 units, and the height (h) is 5 units.

A = 2(4)(3) + 2(4)(5) + 2(3)(5) = 24 + 40 + 30 = 94 square units.

Example of Surface Area of a Pyramid

Now, let's calculate the surface area of a square pyramid. The surface area is calculated as the area of the square base plus the area of the triangular faces.

Surface Area of a Square Pyramid: A = Base Area + 4 × (1/2 × base × slant height)

Suppose the base side (s) is 6 units, and the slant height (l) is 8 units.

Base Area = s² = (6)² = 36 square units.

Lateral Surface Area = 4 × (1/2 × 6 × 8) = 4 × 24 = 96 square units.

Total Surface Area = 36 + 96 = 132 square units.

Important Notes

Understanding surface area is vital in many real-world applications, such as material design and packaging. Using nets helps visualize the surface area, making it easier to understand how each part of a solid contributes to its total surface area.
