5.4 Two-Way Frequency Tables

Big Picture

Two-way frequency tables display the frequency of data points across two categorical variables, helping to identify any potential relationships or patterns between the two variables. This is useful for analyzing how one categorical variable relates to another.

Key Terms

  • Survey: A method for collecting information about individuals' opinions, beliefs, or habits.
  • Data: Information collected to represent real-life situations, often in number form.
  • Frequency Table: A table summarizing a data set by showing how often each value occurs within the set.
  • Categorical Variables: Variables that represent categories or groups, such as gender, type of car, or color.

Example: Two-Way Frequency Table

Consider the following survey data about students' preferences for two types of drinks: Coffee and Tea. The survey is based on their gender, and we want to analyze how many students prefer each drink type, broken down by gender.

Gender Coffee Tea
Male 20 10
Female 15 25

In this table, we can see how many males and females prefer Coffee and Tea. The numbers represent the frequency of students' preferences in each category. For example, 20 males prefer Coffee, while 10 males prefer Tea.

Conclusion

Two-way frequency tables are essential for comparing two categorical variables. They help identify relationships between variables and are a fundamental tool in statistics for analyzing survey or experimental data. In the example above, the table shows how the preference for drinks varies by gender.
