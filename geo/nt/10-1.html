10.1 Definition of Congruence

Big Picture

Congruence refers to the concept of two figures having exactly the same shape and size. The figures are congruent if one can be obtained from the other through rigid transformations such as translation, rotation, or reflection. Congruence is fundamental in geometry as it allows us to compare shapes and study their properties.

Key Terms

  • Congruent: Figures that are identical in shape, size, and measure. They may be rotated, reflected, or translated.
  • Corresponding Angles: Angles in the same position relative to a transversal and parallel lines.
  • Plane: A flat, two-dimensional surface extending infinitely in all directions.
  • Rigid Transformations: Transformations that do not change the size or shape of a figure (e.g., translation, rotation, reflection).
  • Trapezoid: A quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides.
  • Distance: The length of the route between two points.
  • Angles: A geometric figure formed by two rays meeting at a common point (vertex).
  • Corresponding Sides: Sides in the same relative position between two congruent figures.
  • Polygon: A closed figure with at least three straight sides.
  • Rectangle: A parallelogram with four right angles.

Congruence in Geometry

Congruence is a key concept in geometry as it allows us to determine when two figures are identical in all aspects except for their position or orientation. In congruent triangles, for example, the corresponding sides are equal in length, and the corresponding angles are equal in measure. Rigid transformations (such as rotations and reflections) preserve congruence because they do not alter the shape or size of a figure.

Key Takeaways

Congruence is a foundational concept in geometry that ensures two figures are identical in shape and size. Through rigid transformations, we can determine if figures are congruent, regardless of their position or orientation. Understanding congruence helps in comparing shapes, proving theorems, and solving geometric problems.
