3.3 Stratified Sampling

Summary Statement of Topic

How might stratified random sampling be used for choosing representatives in a stratum of the population.

Key Terms

  • Random Sample: A subset of a population for which each member of the population has an equal chance of being chosen
  • Range: The difference between the smallest value and the greatest value in the data set
  • Random Sampling: Choosing representatives by rolling a die, for instance
  • Sample: A specified part of a population, intended to represent the population as a whole
  • Subgroups: Another name for stratum
  • Stratum: A single category or sub-population out of a larger population
  • Stratified Sampling: Choosing a proportional number of representatives from each of a number of subgroups of the initial population
  • Units: The individuals in the population are called units
  • Sample Size: The number of individuals or objects that are surveyed or tested in the study
  • Skew: A trend of data favors one end or the other

Important Info

The formula to calculate the number of members from a given stratum in the sample is:

(# of members in the stratum / total # of members in population) × desired sample size = # of members from stratum

Example of Stratified Sampling

Sample Size: 50 dogs

Given Info:
- 247 Collies
- 138 Pit Bulls
- 96 English Mastiffs
- 172 Blue Heelers
- 222 Welsh Corgis
- Total Size: 875

To calculate the number of Blue Heelers in the desired sample size:

Blue Heelers Proportion = 172 / 875 = 0.197 or 19.7%

0.197 × 50 = 9.85 → Rounds to 10 Blue Heelers in the sample.
