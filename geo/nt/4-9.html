4.9 Coefficient of Variance

Big Picture

The coefficient of variance (CV) is used to compare the relative variability of data sets, especially when the means differ significantly. It is the ratio of the standard deviation to the mean, often expressed as a percentage. A higher CV indicates more variability relative to the mean, while a lower CV suggests less variability.

Key Takeaways

The coefficient of variance is a useful metric for comparing the relative variability between different data sets, especially when their means are not equal. A higher CV indicates greater dispersion relative to the mean, while a lower CV suggests more consistency. It is often used in fields such as finance and research to assess risk or variability.
