8.9 Composite Shapes

Summary Statement of Topic

Composite shapes are formed by combining two or more simple geometric shapes, like rectangles, triangles, and circles. To calculate the area and perimeter of composite shapes, we can break the figure down into simpler components and use known formulas for each shape.

Key Terms

  • Area: The space within the perimeter of a two-dimensional figure.
  • Composite Shape: A shape formed by combining two or more simple shapes.
  • Perimeter: The distance around a shape. The perimeter must have a unit of measurement attached to it.
  • Dimensions: The measurements that define the size of a shape.
  • Hypotenuse: The side opposite the right angle in a right triangle.
  • Congruent: Figures that are identical in size and shape.
  • Height: The perpendicular distance from the base to the opposite vertex in a triangle.
  • Triangle: A polygon with three sides and three angles.
  • Theorem: A statement that can be proven based on logical reasoning and mathematical principles.
  • Rectangle: A parallelogram with four right angles.
  • Square: A parallelogram with four right angles and four congruent sides.
  • Circle: The set of all points that are equidistant from a specific point called the center.
  • Angles: A geometric figure formed by two rays that meet at a vertex.
  • Concentric Circles: Circles that share the same center but have different radii.
  • Center: The central point of a circle, equidistant from all points on the circumference.

Example of Composite Shape Calculation

Consider a composite shape made by combining a rectangle and a triangle. To calculate the area, we break it into smaller, simpler shapes:

Let the rectangle have length = 8 units and width = 4 units, and the triangle has a base of 4 units and height of 3 units.

Area of Rectangle: A = length × width = 8 × 4 = 32 square units.

Area of Triangle: A = (base × height) / 2 = (4 × 3) / 2 = 6 square units.

Total area of the composite shape = Area of Rectangle + Area of Triangle = 32 + 6 = 38 square units.

Example of Perimeter Calculation

Consider the same composite shape: a rectangle with length 8 units and width 4 units, and a triangle with a base of 4 units and height of 3 units.

The perimeter of the composite shape is the sum of the individual perimeters of the rectangle and the triangle. For the rectangle:

Perimeter of Rectangle: P = 2 × (length + width) = 2 × (8 + 4) = 24 units.

For the triangle, we need to find the lengths of all sides. Assuming the triangle is a right triangle:
Use the Pythagorean Theorem: c² = a² + b², where a = 4 and b = 3.

Hypotenuse of Triangle: c = √(4² + 3²) = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5 units.

Perimeter of the composite shape = Perimeter of Rectangle + Perimeter of Triangle = 24 + (4 + 3 + 5) = 36 units.

Important Notes

Composite shapes can be more complex than individual simple shapes, but breaking them down into familiar shapes allows for easier calculations of area, perimeter, and other properties. Understanding how to decompose composite shapes is essential for many geometric applications, from architecture to design.
