7.2 The Empirical Rule

Big Picture

The **Empirical Rule** is a key concept used to estimate the probability of occurrence of data points within certain ranges in a **normal distribution**. It allows us to quickly determine the proportion of data within 1, 2, or 3 standard deviations from the mean.

For a **normal distribution**, the **Empirical Rule** states that: - **68%** of the data falls within **1 standard deviation** from the mean - **95%** of the data falls within **2 standard deviations** - **99.7%** of the data falls within **3 standard deviations**

Key Terms

  • Standard Deviation: A measure of spread in a data set, showing how much the values deviate from the mean.
  • Probability: The chance that something will happen, usually expressed as a fraction, decimal, or percentage.
  • Bell Curve: A symmetrical curve that represents a normal distribution, with most values clustering around the mean.

Important Info

The **Empirical Rule** helps us make quick estimations about data in a **normal distribution**:

- **68%** of data falls between **-1 standard deviation (-1σ)** and **+1 standard deviation (+1σ)** - **95%** of data falls between **-2 standard deviations (-2σ)** and **+2 standard deviations (+2σ)** - **99.7%** of data falls between **-3 standard deviations (-3σ)** and **+3 standard deviations (+3σ)**

Example

Suppose we have a **normal distribution** where the mean height of adult women is **64 inches** and the standard deviation is **3 inches**.

Using the **Empirical Rule**, we can estimate:

- **68%** of women have a height between **61 inches** and **67 inches** (±1σ) 

64 - 3 = 61 inches
64 + 3 = 67 inches
- **95%** of women have a height between **58 inches** and **70 inches** (±2σ) 
64 - 6 = 58 inches
64 + 6 = 70 inches
- **99.7%** of women have a height between **55 inches** and **73 inches** (±3σ) 
64 - 9 = 55 inches
64 + 9 = 73 inches

Conclusion

The **Empirical Rule** provides a fast and useful way to estimate how much of a dataset falls within certain ranges of the mean. For a **normal distribution**, it helps us quickly understand the spread of data and make informed decisions based on probabilities.
