8.7 The Pythagoras Theorem

Summary Statement of Topic

The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse's length is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides' lengths. This fundamental theorem is essential in geometry and trigonometry.

Key Terms

  • Theorem: A statement that has been proven to be true based on previously established statements.
  • Right Triangles: A triangle with exactly one right angle (90°), where the two sides adjacent to the right angle are called legs, and the side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse.
  • Distance: The length of the route between two points.
  • Triangle: A polygon with three sides and three angles.
  • Angles: A geometric figure formed by two rays that connect at a single point (vertex).
  • Opposite: The opposite of a number x is -x; a number and its opposite always sum to zero.
  • Hypotenuse: The side opposite the right angle in a right triangle, always the longest side.
  • Right angle: An angle equal to 90 degrees.
  • Legs: The two sides adjacent to the right angle in a right triangle.
  • Area: The space within the perimeter of a two-dimensional figure.
  • Square: A parallelogram with four equal sides and four right angles.
  • Diagram: A drawing used to represent a mathematical problem.
  • Quadrilateral: A closed figure with four sides and four vertices.
  • Center: The central point of a circle, equidistant from all points on the circle.
  • Straight angle: An angle measuring 180 degrees, appearing as a straight line.

Example of Pythagorean Theorem Calculation

Consider a right-angled triangle with legs of lengths 3 and 4 units. To find the length of the hypotenuse (c), we use the Pythagorean Theorem:

a² + b² = c²
3² + 4² = c²
9 + 16 = c²
25 = c²
c = √25 = 5

Therefore, the length of the hypotenuse is 5 units.

Example (Larger Values)

Consider another right-angled triangle with legs of lengths 7 and 24 units. To find the hypotenuse:

a² + b² = c²
7² + 24² = c²
49 + 576 = c²
625 = c²
c = √625 = 25

Therefore, the length of the hypotenuse is 25 units.

Important Notes

The Pythagorean Theorem only applies to right-angled triangles. It's a fundamental result in geometry that helps solve problems involving distances and triangle properties. Pythagorean triples (sets of whole numbers) are integer solutions to the theorem, such as (3, 4, 5) or (5, 12, 13).
