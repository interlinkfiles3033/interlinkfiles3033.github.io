9.11 Tessellations

Big Picture

A tessellation is a tiling over a plane with one or more figures that fill the plane without any overlaps or gaps. Tessellations can be made using geometric shapes such as hexagons, squares, or triangles. These tilings have been used in art, architecture, and even nature to create visually appealing patterns that repeat endlessly.

Key Terms

  • Plane: A flat, two-dimensional surface that extends infinitely in all directions.
  • Hexagon: A polygon with six sides.
  • Point: A location in space with no size or shape, usually represented as a coordinate on a grid.
  • Angles: The geometric figure formed by two rays meeting at a common point (vertex).
  • Equilateral Triangles: A triangle where all three sides are of equal length, and all angles are 60 degrees.
  • Circle: A set of points in a plane that are all at the same distance from a fixed point, called the center.
  • Interior Angle: The angles inside a polygon formed by two adjacent sides.
  • Octagon: A polygon with eight sides.
  • Pentagon: A polygon with five sides.
  • Square: A quadrilateral with four equal sides and four right angles.

Tessellation in Art and Architecture

Tessellations have been used in art and architecture for centuries. A famous example is the works of Dutch artist M.C. Escher, who created intricate tessellations that feature repeated patterns with mathematical precision. Tessellations also appear in architectural designs, especially in tiled floors and mosaics, where geometric patterns cover entire surfaces.

One common tessellation in architecture is the use of square tiles, which are arranged to fill an area completely with no gaps or overlaps.

Key Takeaways

Tessellations are a powerful way to explore geometric shapes and their properties. By using shapes such as triangles, squares, and hexagons, we can create intricate patterns that fill the entire plane. These patterns are seen everywhere in nature, art, and architecture, demonstrating the beauty and utility of tessellation in mathematics and design.
