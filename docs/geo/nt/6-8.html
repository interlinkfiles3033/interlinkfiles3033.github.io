6.8 Random Variable Variance

Big Picture

**Variance** measures how much the values in a data set deviate from the **mean**. It gives a sense of how spread out the data points are. A high variance indicates that the data points are widely spread, while a low variance means that they are close to the mean.

The formula for variance is: σ² = Σ (xᵢ - μ)² / N where: - \(xᵢ\) represents each individual data point, - \(μ\) is the mean of the data set, - \(N\) is the number of data points.

Key Terms

  • Variable: A symbol representing a changing or unknown quantity. Common variables are \(a\), \(b\), \(x\), \(y\), etc.
  • Arithmetic Mean: The average of a data set, calculated as the sum of all values divided by the number of values.
  • Variance (σ²): The average of the squared differences from the mean, representing how spread out the data points are.
  • Standard Deviation: The square root of the variance, providing a measure of spread that is in the same units as the data.
  • Expected Value: The long-term average or mean of a random variable in probability theory.
  • Data Set: A collection of observations or values of a variable.

Summary

The **variance** of a data set is calculated by taking the mean of the squared differences from the mean. This measure gives an idea of the spread or dispersion of the data.

The **formula for variance** is: σ² = Σ (xᵢ - μ)² / N - Where **μ** is the mean of the data set, - **xᵢ** represents each individual data point, - **N** is the number of data points in the data set.

Example: Data Set

Suppose we have the data set: {2, 4, 6, 8, 10}.

First, calculate the mean (\(μ\)): 

μ = (2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10) / 5
μ = 30 / 5
μ = 6
Next, calculate the squared differences from the mean: 
(2 - 6)² = 16
(4 - 6)² = 4
(6 - 6)² = 0
(8 - 6)² = 4
(10 - 6)² = 16
Finally, calculate the variance: 
σ² = (16 + 4 + 0 + 4 + 16) / 5
σ² = 40 / 5
σ² = 8
So, the variance of this data set is **8**.

Conclusion

The **variance** is a crucial concept in statistics, providing a quantitative measure of the spread of data. A higher variance means more spread out data, while a lower variance indicates that data points are closer to the mean. Understanding variance allows us to make more informed decisions and better interpret the variability in our data.
