8.6 Area and Perimeter of Triangles and Quadrilaterals

Big Picture

The **area** and **perimeter** of shapes are used in everyday life for measuring spaces and determining the boundaries of various objects. Whether you are building a house, designing a garden, or simply measuring a field, understanding how to calculate the area and perimeter is crucial.

Key Terms

  • Distance: The length of the route between two points.
  • Perimeter: The distance around a shape. The perimeter of any figure must have a unit of measurement attached to it. If no specific units are given, write the numeric value followed by “units”.
  • Area: The space within the perimeter of a two-dimensional figure.
  • Rectangle: A parallelogram with four right angles.
  • Parallelogram: A quadrilateral with two pairs of parallel sides.
  • Height: The perpendicular distance from the base of a shape to the opposite vertex.
  • Triangle: A polygon with three sides and three angles.
  • Midpoint: The midpoint of two vectors is the location in the center of their endpoints.
  • Trapezoid: A quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel opposite sides.
  • Congruent: Figures are identical in size, shape, and measure.
  • Square: A parallelogram with four right angles and four congruent sides.

Important Info

- The **perimeter** of any polygon is found by adding the lengths of its sides.
- The **area** of a shape depends on the formula specific to that shape. For example:

  • For a **rectangle**: Area = length × width
  • For a **parallelogram**: Area = base × height
  • For a **triangle**: Area = (base × height) / 2
  • For a **trapezoid**: Area = (b1 + b2) × height / 2
  • For a **square**: Area = side²

Formulas

  • Rectangle: A = bh
  • Parallelogram: A = bh
  • Triangle: A = bh / 2
  • Trapezoid: A = ((b1 + b2)h) / 2
  • Rhombus: A = bh or A = (d1 × d2) / 2
  • Kite: A = (d1 × d2) / 2
  • Square: A = s²

Example

Consider a **rectangle** with a length of **8 cm** and a width of **5 cm**. To find the area, we use the formula: 

Area = length × width
Area = 8 cm × 5 cm
Area = 40 cm²
Therefore, the area of the rectangle is **40 cm²**.

Conclusion

Understanding the **area** and **perimeter** of various shapes is essential for solving real-world problems. These concepts are used in a wide variety of fields, from construction to art. By practicing the formulas for different shapes, you can become proficient in calculating areas and perimeters in everyday situations.
