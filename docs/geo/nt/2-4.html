2.4 Undercoverage

Summary Statement of Topic

How things in real life are supposed to be distributed, but cannot be properly distributed based on a sample scale due to aspects like undercoverage.

Key Terms

  • Distribution: A description of the possible values of a random variable and the possible occurrences of these values
  • Population: The entire group of interest from which the sample is drawn
  • Arithmetic Mean: Also called the average; Arithmetic Mean = x₁ + x₂ + ... + xn
  • Average: Also called the arithmetic mean
  • Skewed/Skew: A given set means to cause the trend of data to favor one end or the other
  • Data: Information that has been collected to represent real life situations, usually in number form
  • Poll: Question members of a group regarding a specific topic
  • Margin of Error: A percentage by which the true number for the entire population may differ
  • Survey: A data collection method used to gather information about individuals’ opinions, beliefs, or habits
  • Sample: A specified part of a population, intended to represent the population as a whole
  • Bias: Desire to achieve a specific result from a particular study, regardless of the data
  • Undercoverage: Describes a sample with too few members of a given group or demographic
  • Self-selection: Self-selected respondents tend to have stronger opinions and are more motivated to respond, potentially causing bias
  • Estimate: An approximate answer that is reasonable or makes sense given the problem
  • Non-Response Bias: Limitations on voters who don’t have enough time or inclination to respond to a survey
  • Sample size: The number of individuals, or objects, that are surveyed or tested in the study
  • Convenience Sampling: Refers to the process of choosing a sample based on members who are easily accessible
  • Biased: When a sample ends up with one or more sub-groups that are either significantly over-represented or under-represented
  • Statistics: The branch of mathematics that involves collecting, analyzing, and displaying data
  • Range: The difference between the smallest value and the greatest value in a data set

Examples from Practice

  • Pretest among a smaller subset of the target respondents is a useful method for checking a questionnaire.
  • In sampling bias, undercoverage is often a problem with convenience samples.
  • The bias that results from a non-representative sample is called selection bias.
  • Stratified randomization is attained by executing a separate randomization procedure within each of two or more subsets of participants.
  • If Mᵢ, the number of observations within each cluster is known, then M = M₁ + M₂.
  • Possible response lists should always be mutually exclusive.
  • Non-Response Bias occurs when the observed value deviates from the population parameter due to differences between the respondent and non-respondent.
  • Non-probability sampling method(s) is/are convenience sampling.

Conclusion

Understanding the causes and effects of undercoverage in sampling can help improve the accuracy and representativeness of survey results. Identifying potential biases and applying proper sampling techniques are crucial for reliable data collection.
