4.1 Arithmetic Mean

Summary Statement of Topic

An arithmetic mean is used to give an overall summary of an aspect of the population or sample population. It is often referred to as the "average."

Key Terms

  • Data: Information that has been collected to represent real life situations, usually in number form.
  • Average: The arithmetic mean is often called the average.
  • Arithmetic Mean: Also called the Average; Arithmetic Mean = (x₁ + x₂ + ... + xn) / n.
  • Geometric Mean: A method of finding the “middle” value in a set that contains values with differing levels of influence.
  • Harmonic Mean: Calculated by dividing the number of values in the set by the sum of the inverses of the values in the set.
  • Statistics: A branch of mathematics that involves collecting, analyzing, and displaying data.
  • Units: The individuals in the population.
  • Population Mean: The mean of all of the members of an entire population.
  • Sample Mean: The mean of only the members of a sample or subset of a population.
  • Population: The entire group of interest from which the sample is drawn.
  • Sample: A specified part of a population, intended to represent the population as a whole.
  • Interval: A specific and limited part of a function.

Example

Find the mean of the following data:

22, 30, 24, 42, 24, 42, 18, 32, 24, 48, 24, 45, 28, 46, 22, 42

First, sum the numbers:
22 + 30 + 24 + 42 + 24 + 42 + 18 + 32 + 24 + 48 + 24 + 45 + 28 + 46 + 22 + 42 = 513
Then divide by the total number of values (16):
513 ÷ 16 = 32.06 (rounded).

Additional Notes

The arithmetic mean is the most common measure of central tendency and is useful in summarizing the overall "average" of a dataset. However, it can be affected by extreme values (outliers) in the dataset.
