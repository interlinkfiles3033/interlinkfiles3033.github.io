7.1 Understanding Normal Distribution

Big Picture

**Normal distribution** is a way to represent a **continuous random variable**. It is symmetric and follows the shape of a bell curve, where most data points cluster around the mean, and the probability of extreme values (either high or low) decreases as we move further from the mean.

A **normal distribution** is fully described by two parameters: - **Mean (μ)**: the center of the distribution - **Standard Deviation (σ)**: the spread of the distribution

The **Empirical Rule** states that for a normal distribution: - 68% of the data lies within 1 standard deviation from the mean - 95% lies within 2 standard deviations - 99.7% lies within 3 standard deviations

Key Terms

  • Standard Deviation: A measure of the spread of a data set. The larger the standard deviation, the more spread out the data is.
  • Distribution: A description of the possible values of a random variable and the likelihood of these values.
  • Percentile: A data value where a specified percentage of the data lies below it.
  • Range: The difference between the smallest and largest values in a data set.
  • Population: The entire group of interest from which a sample may be drawn.
  • Maximum: The largest number in a data set.

Summary

The **normal distribution** is a fundamental concept in statistics. It is symmetric, bell-shaped, and described by the **mean** and **standard deviation**. The **Empirical Rule** provides a quick way to estimate the proportion of values within certain ranges:

- **68%** of values fall within **1 standard deviation** from the mean - **95%** of values fall within **2 standard deviations** - **99.7%** of values fall within **3 standard deviations**

This distribution is used widely in various fields such as economics, biology, psychology, and more.

Example: Normal Distribution

Suppose the heights of adult women in a population are normally distributed with a mean height of 64 inches and a standard deviation of 3 inches. We can use the **Empirical Rule** to answer the following:

- **68%** of women will have heights between: 

64 - 3 = 61 inches
64 + 3 = 67 inches
So, 68% of women will have heights between **61 inches and 67 inches**.

- **95%** of women will have heights between: 
64 - 6 = 58 inches
64 + 6 = 70 inches
So, 95% of women will have heights between **58 inches and 70 inches**.

- **99.7%** of women will have heights between: 
64 - 9 = 55 inches
64 + 9 = 73 inches
So, 99.7% of women will have heights between **55 inches and 73 inches**.

Conclusion

**Normal distribution** is a key concept in statistics, providing a way to represent continuous random variables. The **Empirical Rule** is an important tool for estimating the spread of data in a normal distribution. By understanding these concepts, we can make more informed decisions and predictions based on statistical data.
